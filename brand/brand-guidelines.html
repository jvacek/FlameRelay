01 — Foundation

What we're doing here

Positioning Statement

LitRoute is the world's most low-stakes social network — for lighter-passers, note-leavers, and anyone who believes a small object can carry something worth saying. Unlike social media that performs connection or journaling apps that keep it locked away, LitRoute passes the story forward, one hand at a time.

Brand Promise

Every lighter has a story. We help you find yours.

Core Values

Playfulness

Silliness is the whole point. Being weird about a lighter is correct behavior here. We don't need a serious reason.

Radical Transparency

No accounts, no tracking, open source. We don't just say it — we prove it, so you can check for yourself.

Human Warmth

A stranger's note at the right moment can genuinely change your day. That's not nothing. We take that seriously.

Low Stakes, High Meaning

This doesn't have to matter. That's exactly why it does. The best parts of life are often the ones nobody planned.

02 — Audience

Who's holding the lighter

The Accidental Romantic

They didn't look for LitRoute. It found them — tucked inside a lighter someone handed them at a show, or mentioned by a friend who thought it was hilarious. They're the kind of person who reads the back of cereal boxes, keeps a pebble from a meaningful trip, and quietly loves small unexpected connections.

They're not building their personal brand. They just found a thing, thought "huh, that's kinda neat", and now they're leaving a note for the next stranger. They'd absolutely write something good.

Goals: Participate in something small and human without any fuss or sign-up friction.
Pain points: Every app wants their data, their email, their time. This one doesn't.

Logo direction

Form: Icon + Wordmark. The name "LitRoute" is strong enough to carry a wordmark on its own, but there's a genuinely compelling icon concept here — so we recommend a combination mark where both can work independently.

Symbol concept: the flame-pin. Take the familiar teardrop shape of a map location pin. Replace the dot at its center with a simple flame — not a stylized cartoon flame, but a single, clean organic curve suggesting fire. The result is a shape that reads immediately as both "you are here" and "something is burning here." It encodes both ideas — route and flame — without explaining either. The icon should be simple enough to work as a favicon at 16×16px and as an embossed mark on a lighter. Test both.

Typographic treatment: Set "LitRoute" in Fraunces Bold, optical size ~14–20 depending on application. Tracking at default or slightly tight (−10 units). The capital L and R naturally create two visual anchors; don't disrupt that. Consider a subtle color split: "Lit" in Amber Gold, "Route" in Char — reinforcing the two-concept name without adding any decorative elements.

What to avoid

No gradients, glows, or flame effects on the logotype — the warmth comes from the color palette, not from visual effects. Avoid anything that reads "crypto project" or "extreme sports app"; this is a quiet, personal thing. And please, no literal lighters in the logo — the brand is about the journey, not the object. The pin already tells that story.

04 — Voice & Tone

How we talk

The key principle: UI copy is precise and invisible. Long-form copy is where the personality lives. Don't try to be quirky in a button label. Save it for the about page.

Voice Traits

Trait We are… We are not…
Precise Clear and direct. Every word earns its place in UI copy. Terse or robotic. Clarity doesn't mean cold.
Warm Genuinely caring. We want your note to reach the right person. Saccharine or performative. We don't do "we're a family" energy.
Playful Weird in the best way. Comfortable with the silliness of this whole thing. Trying to be funny. The humor comes from being earnest, not from trying.
Honest Transparent about what this is. One person made this for fun. That's it. Overselling. We don't call it a "platform" or a "community" unless it earns those words.

Tone by Context

Navigation & UI

Ultra-short. Action-oriented. No personality — just clarity. "Check in." "Leave a note." "View route." Every extra word is friction.

Onboarding & About

Full quirk mode. This is where you explain what this is and why it exists. Be self-aware, funny, a little absurd. Let Jonas's voice through.

Error Messages

Brief but human. Acknowledge what went wrong, don't catastrophize, add a tiny amount of warmth. "Something went sideways. Try again?" is fine.

Confirmation & Empty States

A small moment of delight is allowed here. "This lighter's just getting started." beats "No check-ins yet." Don't miss these small opportunities.

Writing Principles

In UI copy, one word beats three. In storytelling, take your time.
✗ "Submit your check-in entry to the lighter's journey log"
✓ "Check in"
The lighter is the hero. Not the app.
✗ "LitRoute lets you track where your lighter has been."
✓ "Your lighter's been places. Let's see where."
Never sound like a startup. You're one person who made a cool thing.
✗ "We're building the future of social object tracking."
✓ "I'm funemployed and I made this because I thought it would be funny. It kind of is."
Privacy is a value, not a feature. Live it; don't brag about it.
✗ "We're privacy-first! Zero data collection! Your info is safe with us!"
✓ "No account needed. We genuinely don't want your data."
Write notes like you're leaving them for a friend you haven't met yet.
✗ "Add a message for the next user of this item."
✓ "Say something to whoever picks this up next."
